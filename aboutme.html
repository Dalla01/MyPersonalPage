About me

Hello everyone! My name is André Garcia and here's a little bit about my academic education and qualification. In the end of 2018, Agronomist graduated from Instituto Federal do Espírito Santo - IFES Campus Santa Teresa, where I was developed projects and researches related to irrigation management and agrometeorology, becoming a member of the research group IRRIFES lead by Dr. Gustavo Haddad, which focuses on studies related to irrigation and water resources. Still in graduation, I had the opportunity to do an internship at a meteorological microstation in which I was responsible for processing and organizing the database, as well as providing it to users. In 2021 I was concluded my Master's degree at the Graduate Program in Vegetal Production at the Universidade Estadual do Norte Fluminense Darcy Ribeiro - UENF, in the concentration area of irrigated agriculture and had agrometeorology as research line, surpervised by Dr. José Carlos Mendonça. My research focused in verify the feasilbility of Conilon coffee cultivation in the Northern of Rio de Janeiro based on drip irrigation and climate parameters. Between 2019 and 2021, I carried out a Specialization in Occupational Safety, in which I developed a research to identify occupational risk areas in rural properties using remote sensing images. Since 2021 to currently, I'm PhD candidate at Instituto Nacional de Pesquisas Espaciais - INPE, where develop research about agricultural remote sense surpervised by Dr Ieda Sanches, and integrate the Agricultural Remote Sensing Laboratory - AgriRS. Basically my study focus on irrigated rice mapping using optical and microwave (SAR) sensors. In the first quarter of 2023 I became a member of International Society for Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing - ISPRS.
